What Do You Need To Play?

  1. Game Master
    The person who creates the world, story, and adventures, manages the process, and runs the game.
  2. Players
    People who take part in the game and play their Characters. The game follows their point of view, and the Game Master leads them through the adventure.
  3. Dice
    You can use your own check systems or online dice rollers, but physical dice are strongly recommended.
  4. Optional tools for immersion:
    1. Step away from the outside world
      The game works better if everyone focuses on it for a few hours. Do not overdo this, but do not ignore focus either.
    2. Table and chairs
      It is easier to stay focused when everyone sits at the same table instead of lying around and slowly falling asleep.
    3. Game Master separation
      The Game Master should usually sit or stand apart from the players, or use a GM screen. This helps separate the GM role from the player side of the table.
    4. Character sheets
      Physical or printed Character sheets help immersion. A character image also helps players picture and connect with their character.
    5. Miniatures, maps, and similar tools
      Miniatures, maps, and fields can help players understand scenes, especially combat. They do not need to be expensive or detailed. Dice, small objects, or hand-drawn maps are enough.
